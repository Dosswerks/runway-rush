Runway Rush

Get them there on time.

The Story

It is the busiest travel day of the year, and the airport is in full chaos. Flights are boarding, gates are closing, and five passengers are about to miss their departures. Crowds pack the terminal concourse, luggage carts weave through the halls, and security teams patrol every corridor.

Beyond the terminal doors, the runway is even more dangerous. Service trucks barrel across the tarmac, baggage trains snake between gates, and massive airplanes taxi into position with no intention of slowing down.

Somewhere in the middle of it all stands you -- the only person who can help.

Each passenger has a gate to catch and a plane to board. Guide them through the crowded terminal, across the active runway, and safely to their departure gate before time runs out. One wrong step and they are back at the start. Move too slowly and the flight leaves without them.

Five passengers. Five gates. One shot to get them all there on time.

How to Play

On desktop, use the arrow keys to move your passenger one lane at a time -- up, down, left, or right. On mobile, swipe in any direction to move.

Your goal is to guide each passenger from the bottom of the screen, through the terminal and across the runway, to their assigned departure gate at the top. The target gate is shown in the "NOW DEPARTING" banner above the game.

The Airport

The lower half of the screen is the terminal concourse. Crowds of travelers, security carts, and luggage carriers move back and forth across the lanes. Avoid them all -- any collision sends your passenger back to the start.

In the middle is the Security Checkpoint -- a safe zone where you can pause and plan your next move.

The upper half is the active runway. Service trucks and airplanes are lethal on contact. Baggage trains, marked with yellow and black hazard stripes, are the only safe way across -- jump on one and ride it across the tarmac, then hop off toward your gate. But if you step onto a runway lane without landing on a baggage train, you will not last long.

Scoring

Each passenger delivered earns points, with bonus time adding to your score. Collect the bonus items scattered across the field for extra points.

After all five passengers board, you will see your NPS (Net Promoter Score) -- a percentage that measures how efficiently you moved your passengers. Fewer moves means a higher NPS. A perfect run with no wasted steps scores 100%. The goal is to find the most direct route while dodging everything in your path.

Tips

Watch the lanes carefully before moving. Each lane moves at a different speed and direction.

On the runway, time your jump onto a baggage train -- do not step into a hazard lane without one.

The Security Checkpoint is your friend. Use it to pause and read the runway traffic before committing.

Efficiency matters. Every extra move lowers your NPS. Plan your route and move with purpose.

The gates are closing. The passengers are counting on you. Welcome to Runway Rush.
